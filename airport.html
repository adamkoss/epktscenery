Katowice-Pyrzowice International Airport
(Katowice Airport)
Katowice-Pyrzowice International Airport (short: Katowice Airport), ICAO code: EPKT, IATA code: KTW. Runways: 09-27, concrete, 3200m (10499ft)x45m (148ft)

Katowice-Pyrzowice International Airport is localised beside Pyrzowice village and about 30 km form Katowice city - a capital of Upper Silesia voivodeship. The airport is one of fiveteen international airports in Poland. The airport operates:
  • 1 national flight (Warsaw)
  • 41 international flights
  • 49 charter flights
  • 7 cargo flights
... and airlines:
  • regular: Wizzair, PLL LOT, Eurowings, Lufthansa, Ryanair, Ellianair
  • charter: Enterair, Small Planet Airlines, Corendon Airlines, TUIfly, Bulgarian Air Charter, Air Cairo, Pegasus, Nouvelair, Freebird, Arkia, Travel Service,Tunisair, El Al
  • cargo: DHL, FedEx, Farnair, Air Contractors, Sprint Air, TNT
History of Katowice Airport begins in World War II. In 1940 III Reich army has created airfield beside Pyrzowice village. After war, the airfield was using by Soviet Union army, until 1951. Then, the airport took over by polish army. In 1966, first civil passanger plane departured from this civil-militaty airport. The civil company GTL SA (pol. Górnośląskie Towarzystwo Lotnicze S.A.) has been managed airport since 2000.
